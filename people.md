## Coordination & Management

- **Project Coordinator (PC)** – Prof. Nacim Ramdani (Université d’Orléans, UOR)
- **Project Manager (PM)** – To be appointed
- **Exploitation Manager** – Dr. Stamatia Rizou (SingularLogic, SLG)
- **Dissemination & Communication Leader** – Dr. Mathaios Panteli (University of Cyprus, UCY)

The Project Coordinator leads the Supervisory Board and oversees the overall scientific and training programme.
The Project Manager supports day-to-day management, coordinates recruitment and maintains the website and online
presence. The Exploitation Manager chairs the Dissemination, Exploitation and IPR Board, while the Dissemination &
Communication Leader coordinates communication and outreach activities.

## Beneficiaries

These are the SPRING beneficiaries (PhD-recruiting institutions):

- **UOR – Université d’Orléans (France)**  
  PRISME Research Center – Project Coordinator; systems and control, cyber-physical systems, resilience of infrastructures.

- **UOL – Carl von Ossietzky Universität Oldenburg (Germany)**  
  Department of Computing Science – formal methods, cyber-physical systems, smart energy and transportation.

- **UNIBI – Universität Bielefeld (Germany)**  
  Center for Cognitive Interaction Technology – machine learning, explainable AI, data-driven resilience.

- **UiO – University of Oslo (Norway)**  
  Department of Informatics – formal methods, verification and software engineering for safety-critical CPS.

- **TUG – Graz University of Technology (Austria)**  
  Institute for Information Processing and Communication – verification, shielding for learning-enabled systems, transport.

- **UCY – University of Cyprus (Cyprus)**  
  KIOS Research and Innovation Center of Excellence – critical infrastructures, water and energy systems, testbeds.

- **SLG – SingularLogic S.A. (Greece)**  
  European Projects Department – smart cities, ICT platforms, digital services, industrial partner and exploitation manager.

- **8BEL – Eight Bells Ltd (Cyprus)**  
  R&D Department – cyber-security, networking and IoT solutions, non-academic beneficiary.

- **HMT – Humatects GmbH (Germany)**  
  R&D Department – intelligent transportation systems, human factors and simulation tools.

SPRING is supported by a strong network of associated academic and industrial partners, which contribute
use cases, secondments, testbeds and training:

- **SIEMENS – Siemens Mobility GmbH (Germany)** – transportation systems and testbeds.
- **SINTEF – SINTEF AS (Norway)** – research and datasets in transportation and CPS.
- **AVL – AVL List GmbH (Austria)** – automotive and transportation R&D, testbeds.
- **EAC – Electricity Authority of Cyprus (Cyprus)** – smart grids and power systems.
- **HRI – Honda Research Institute Europe GmbH (Germany)** – intelligent transportation and energy use cases.
- **EDF – Électricité de France (France)** – large-scale energy systems and utilities.
- **CNRS – Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique (France)** – academic research expertise.
- **Science Tokyo – Institute of Science Tokyo (Japan)** – academic partner in formal methods and CPS.
- **SPH – Space Hellas S.A. (Greece)** – telecommunications, ICT and security services.
- **SONE – Sense One Technologies (Greece)** – IoT platforms, smart buildings and energy services.
